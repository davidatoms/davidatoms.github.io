Google Acquires Wiz for $23 Billion

Announced: July 2024
Deal Type: Full Acquisition


Deal Overview

Google announced today that it has entered into an agreement to acquire cloud-security start-up Wiz in a cash-and-stock transaction valued at approximately $23 billion. Founded in 2020, Wiz has quickly become one of the fastest-growing security platforms in the public-cloud space, boasting more than 40% of the Fortune 100 as customers.

Strategic Rationale

The acquisition bolsters Google Cloud Platform's zero-trust security portfolio and helps it compete head-to-head with AWS and Microsoft in the lucrative enterprise market. Wiz's agentless architecture also complements Google's BeyondCorp framework and Chronicle threat-detection products.

Key Highlights

  • Largest cyber-security acquisition since Broadcom–Symantec (2019)
  • Accelerates Google's push into multi-cloud workload protection
  • Wiz founders Dor & Ami will join Google Cloud's security leadership team
  • Transaction expected to close in Q4 2024, pending regulatory approval

Industry Impact

The deal is expected to trigger further consolidation in the cloud-security market as hyperscalers seek differentiated offerings. Rival vendors such as Palo Alto Networks and CrowdStrike could see heightened M&A interest.

Conclusion

Google's purchase of Wiz underscores the strategic importance of cloud-native security and signals the company's commitment to safeguarding enterprise workloads at scale.
